Ludus

Ludus is a side game for nobles building a town that is focused on resource growth and management. With each event a player attends, their town will produce resources. These resources can be used to produce items, earn money, or mobilize the noble's armed forces. Resources can also be invested back into various projects to make them more efficient or effective. There are three main areas: production, economy, and military. The Noble can invest directly into the area or invest in a project in one of those areas.

*All throwing weapons deal 1 less damage if used with a talent. Unless otherwise clarified, Ranger, Marksmen, and Stalker skills and abilities must be used with ranged weapons.

Arrows

Arrows are automatically retrieved after an encounter unless otherwise specified. Rangers can make free wood arrows at rest, replenishing their stock. Any non-rangers who use a bow and arrows will have a quiver as well; however, all arrows will be shoddily crafted and add no damage to their shots.

Ranger Arrow Types:
  • Not in the Guild - Arrows are made out of wood and add 1 base damage to their shots.
  • In the Guild - Arrows are made of bronze and add 2 base damage to their shots.
  • In either of the Factions - Arrows are made of steel and add 3 base damage to their shots.

*This "class" is not official and was a wonderful April Fools joke by our GMs and largely made by Chase Harvey.

Commoners are less capable of withstanding mortal wounds, as such they start with 1 HP instead of 3 HP. Commoners do not gain experience but can instead expend Effort to get their abilities and skills. The player can get 1 Effort per downtime spent on learning a specific talent. The Effort costs of talents follow the normal XP costs (ie. Abilities cost 5). Effort acquired for 1 talent cannot be used for a different one. Commoners do not start with a weapon dexterity, armor or weapons. Commoners start with 10 Iron.

Warrior Titles

A warrior can earn up to 5 titles. Each warrior can eventually get 1 major title and can have 2 minor ones active at a time. The major title can go up to R3 and is mostly permanent. Minor titles can only get to R2 and may be switched out at a rest/in town. R1s are free, R2s and R3s are 5 XP. The warrior must accomplish a task to earn each rank. Warriors start with 1 active slot and gets another active when they join the guild and another active when they join the faction.
